An AI-driven research framework for hardware-aware Quantum Error Correction (QEC). The project trains explicit surrogate noise models on simulated and empirical hardware data to replace idealized i.i.d. noise assumptions with physical reality, specifically targeting Penning trap ion crystal architectures.

### Key Highlights
- **Surrogate Noise Modeling**: Trains neural networks on physical noise data from Penning trap simulations to build accurate, hardware-specific noise models.
- **Decoder Integration**: Implements and benchmarks ML-based syndrome decoders (e.g., transformer-based) against classical MWPM decoders under realistic noise conditions.
- **Scalable Architecture**: Designed to scale across different QEC codes (surface codes, color codes) and hardware platforms.
